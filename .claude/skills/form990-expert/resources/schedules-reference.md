# Form 990 Schedules Reference

Complete guide to Form 990 schedules - when required, what they contain, and how to interpret them.

## Schedule A: Public Charity Status and Public Support

**Required**: All 501(c)(3) organizations (except private foundations)

**Purpose**: Proves the organization qualifies as a public charity (not a private foundation)

### Public Charity Tests

| Part | Test | Qualification |
|------|------|---------------|
| II | 170(b)(1)(A)(vi) | Churches, schools, hospitals, government units |
| III | 509(a)(1) | Public support test - 1/3 support from public sources |
| III | 509(a)(2) | Gross receipts test - <1/3 from investments, >1/3 from exempt activities |
| IV | Supporting org | Supports other public charities (Type I, II, or III) |

### Public Support Calculation (Parts II-III)

Five-year rolling calculation:
- **Numerator**: Public support (contributions from individuals, government grants)
- **Denominator**: Total support (all contributions + investment income)
- **Threshold**: Must be >33.33% (or meet facts-and-circumstances test at 10%)

### Key Lines
| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Part II, Line 14 | Public support percentage |
| Part III, Line 17 | Investment income percentage |
| Part IV | Supporting organization details |

**Red flags**:
- Public support dropping below 33%
- Heavy reliance on a single donor
- Unusual spikes in one year

## Schedule B: Schedule of Contributors

**Required**: Organizations receiving contributions from any donor >$5,000 (or >2% of total contributions)

**Note**: Donor names are confidential and redacted from public copies

### Information Collected
| Column | Data |
|--------|------|
| (a) | Contributor number |
| (b) | Name, address, SSN/EIN |
| (c) | Total contributions |
| (d) | Type of contribution (cash, noncash, art) |

**Useful for**: Understanding donor concentration (even without names, you can see contribution amounts)

## Schedule C: Political Campaign and Lobbying Activities

**Required**: 501(c) organizations with political or lobbying activities

### Parts
| Part | Activity |
|------|----------|
| I-A | Political campaign activities (501(c)(3) prohibited!) |
| I-B | Political expenditures (501(c)(4), (5), (6) orgs) |
| II-A | Lobbying activities (501(h) election) |
| II-B | Lobbying activities (no election) |
| III | Political activity and lobbying proxy tax |
| IV | Supplemental information |

**Red flags for 501(c)(3)**:
- Any "Yes" to Part I-A questions = potential revocation
- Lobbying expenses >$1M or >20% of exempt activities

## Schedule G: Supplemental Information Regarding Fundraising

**Required**: Organizations with professional fundraisers, special events, or gaming

### Parts
| Part | Activity | Threshold |
|------|----------|-----------|
| I | Fundraising activities | Using professionals |
| II | Fundraising events | Gross receipts >$15,000 |
| III | Gaming | Gross receipts >$15,000 |

### Part I: Professional Fundraisers
Lists each fundraiser with:
- Name and address
- Activity description
- Retained by fundraiser vs. organization
- Gross receipts
- Amount paid to fundraiser

**Red flag**: Fundraiser retaining >50% of gross receipts

### Part II: Fundraising Events
Top 3 events by gross receipts:
- Event description
- Gross receipts
- Contributions portion
- Non-contribution revenue
- Direct expenses

**Key metric**: Net from events / Gross receipts

## Schedule J: Compensation Information

**Required**: Organizations reporting compensation >$150,000 in Part VII

### Parts
| Part | Content |
|------|---------|
| I | Questions regarding compensation |
| II | Officers, directors, trustees, key employees, highest compensated |
| III | Supplemental information |

### Part I Key Questions
| Line | Question | Red Flag If |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1a | First-class travel | Yes without policy |
| 1b | Travel for companions | Yes without business purpose |
| 2 | Expense discretionary account | Yes without accountability |
| 3 | Housing allowance/residence | Yes without documentation |
| 4a | Club dues | Yes if personal clubs |
| 4b | Personal services | Yes without business purpose |
| 5 | Process for setting compensation | No documented process |
| 6 | Compensation contingent on revenues | Yes (unusual for nonprofits) |
| 7 | Non-fixed payments | Yes without explanation |
| 8 | Initial contract exception | Used frequently |
| 9 | Rebuttable presumption procedure | No (should use) |

### Part II Columns
| Column | Data |
|--------|------|
| (B)(i) | Base compensation |
| (B)(ii) | Bonus and incentive |
| (B)(iii) | Other reportable compensation |
| (C) | Retirement and deferred |
| (D) | Nontaxable benefits |
| (E) | Total (B)(i) through (D) |
| (F) | Compensation from related orgs |

## Schedule K: Supplemental Information on Tax-Exempt Bonds

**Required**: Organizations with outstanding tax-exempt bonds

Detailed information on each bond issue:
- Issue date, amount, maturity
- Purpose
- Private business use
- Arbitrage rebate
- Procedures to monitor compliance

## Schedule L: Transactions with Interested Persons

**Required**: Organizations with transactions involving interested persons

### Parts
| Part | Transaction Type |
|------|------------------|
| I | Excess benefit transactions |
| II | Loans to/from interested persons |
| III | Grants to interested persons |
| IV | Business transactions with interested persons |

### Interested Persons Include
- Current/former officers, directors, trustees
- Key employees
- Substantial contributors (>$5,000 or 2%)
- Family members of above
- 35%-controlled entities of above

**Red flags**:
- Loans to officers without proper documentation
- Above-market payments to related parties
- Undisclosed conflicts of interest

## Schedule N: Liquidation, Termination, Dissolution, or Significant Disposition

**Required**: Organizations that liquidated, terminated, dissolved, or disposed >25% of assets

### Parts
| Part | Activity |
|------|----------|
| I | Liquidation, termination, or dissolution |
| II | Sale, exchange, disposition, or transfer >25% of assets |

### Information Required
- Date of action
- Description of assets disposed
- Fair market value
- Recipient organizations
- Statement of exempt purpose served

## Schedule O: Supplemental Information

**Required**: All organizations (even if no additional information)

**Purpose**: Narrative explanations for any questions requiring additional space

### Common Uses
- Extended mission statement
- Explanation of significant changes
- Description of additional programs (Part III)
- Governance policy details (Part VI)
- Expense allocation methodology (Part IX)
- Compensation determination process (Part VII)
- Geographic service areas

**Useful for**: Understanding context behind numbers, identifying additional programs
