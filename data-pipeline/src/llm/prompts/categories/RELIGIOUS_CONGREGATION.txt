# CATEGORY: RELIGIOUS_CONGREGATION
# DESCRIPTION: Mosques, churches, synagogues, community centers

## WORLD-CLASS BENCHMARKS
Compare this charity against these best-in-class religious congregations:
- Archdiocese of Chicago: Large scale, comprehensive services, 2.2M members, professional administration
- LDS Church (local wards): Highly organized, member engagement, comprehensive programs
- Willow Creek/Saddleback: Megachurch model, innovative programming, high engagement
- Park East Synagogue: Historic institution, comprehensive Jewish programming

## SCORING CALIBRATION FOR RELIGIOUS CONGREGATIONS

### Systemic Leverage (0-30)
- 26-30 (WORLD CLASS): Regional influence, training other mosques, developing curriculum, network hub
- 20-25 (NATIONAL): Multi-site operations, influencing regional religious practice
- 12-19 (REGIONAL): Established congregation with community programs beyond worship
- 6-11 (LOCAL): Standard mosque/worship center, basic programming
- 0-5 (MINIMAL): Prayer space only, no additional programs

### Ummah Gap (0-20) - 3 Sub-Components

**Replaceability (0-8)** - If this mosque disappeared, how hard to replace?
- 7-8: Only Islamic center in area, regional hub
- 5-6: Unique programs not offered elsewhere
- 3-4: Other mosques in area
- 1-2: Many mosques serving same community

**Ummah Relevance (0-6)** - Does this serve the Muslim community?
- 5-6: Primary Islamic institution for Muslim community
- 3-4: Serves Muslims among others
- 1-2: Limited Muslim-specific services

**Funding Gap (0-6)** - Is this underfunded by Muslim donors?
- 5-6: Innovative programs (vs. traditional mosque services)
- 3-4: Moderate funding for religious services
- 1-2: Well-funded congregation

### Absorptive Capacity (0-25) - 2 Sub-Components

**Governance (0-15)** - Board independence, audits, accountability
- 13-15: CN Accountability 95+, professional administration, strong governance
- 10-12: CN Accountability 85-94, good controls, full-time staff
- 7-9: CN Accountability 70-84, part-time staff, basic governance
- 4-6: Volunteer-run, minimal governance
- 0-3: Weak accountability

**Deployment Capacity (0-10)** - Revenue (0-4) + Geographic (0-3) + Zakat (0-3)
- Revenue: $100M+=4, $25-100M=3, $5-25M=2, $1-5M=1, <$1M=0
- Geographic: Regional hub=2, Multi-program=1, Local only=0
- Zakat: Mosques can administer zakat (2-3 typical)

### Evidence of Impact (0-25) - 3 Sub-Components

**Output Tracking (0-8)** - Do they count what they do?
- 7-8: Member engagement dashboards, program tracking
- 5-6: Annual reports with attendance, programs
- 3-4: Basic attendance estimates
- 1-2: Anecdotal descriptions
- 0: No metrics

**Outcome Measurement (0-10)** - Community outcomes measured?
- 9-10: Community surveys, program impact measured
- 7-8: Member engagement outcomes tracked
- 5-6: Some outcome indicators
- 3-4: Attendance only, no outcome measurement
- 0-2: No outcome tracking

**Third-Party Verification (0-7)** - Is there external validation?
- 6-7: Independent evaluations, community surveys
- 4-5: External reviews
- 2-3: Standard financial audit only
- 0-1: Limited external review

## CRITICAL CALIBRATION

**Mosques should be benchmarked against Catholic/LDS/Megachurch standards:**
- Archdiocese of Chicago: $1B+ in assets, comprehensive social services, professional staff
- LDS Ward: Systematic member care, tithing systems, clear governance
- Megachurch: Innovative programming, high engagement, professional production

**Typical mosque scores:**
- Small community mosque: 50-65 total
- Established regional mosque: 65-80 total
- Major Islamic center with comprehensive programs: 75-88 total

**Only score 26-30 on Systemic Leverage if:**
- Training other mosque leaders at scale
- Developing curriculum/programs adopted by other mosques
- Significant regional or national influence
