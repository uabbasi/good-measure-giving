## WORLD-CLASS BENCHMARKS
Compare this charity against these best-in-class philanthropic foundations:
- Ford Foundation: $16 billion endowment, global grantmaking, field-building
- MacArthur Foundation: $8 billion endowment, innovative programs (Genius Grants)
- Lilly Endowment: Major religious giving, community development

## SCORING CALIBRATION FOR PHILANTHROPY/GRANTMAKING ORGS

### Systemic Leverage (0-30)
- 26-30 (WORLD CLASS): Field-building, creating new philanthropic infrastructure, influencing giving patterns at scale
- 20-25 (NATIONAL): Major grantmaking ($50M+/year), supporting ecosystem of organizations
- 12-19 (REGIONAL): Established grantmaking, supporting multiple organizations
- 6-11 (LOCAL): Small-scale grantmaking, limited recipients
- 0-5 (MINIMAL): Occasional grants, pass-through funding only

### Ummah Gap (0-20) - 3 Sub-Components

**Replaceability (0-8)** - If this foundation disappeared, how hard to replace?
- 7-8: Building new philanthropic infrastructure (Pillars Fund model)
- 5-6: Funding causes others don't (climate, mental health)
- 3-4: Standard grantmaking with alternatives
- 1-2: Many foundations in space

**Ummah Relevance (0-6)** - Does this disproportionately affect Muslims?
- 5-6: Building Muslim philanthropic capacity, funding Muslim-led orgs
- 3-4: Grantmaking affecting Muslims among others
- 1-2: General philanthropy

**Funding Gap (0-6)** - Is this underfunded by Muslim donors?
- 5-6: Muslim philanthropic infrastructure (vs. direct service)
- 3-4: Moderate funding for grantmaking
- 1-2: Well-funded philanthropic space

### Absorptive Capacity (0-25) - 2 Sub-Components

**Governance (0-15)** - Board independence, audits, accountability
- 13-15: CN Accountability 95+, professional grantmaking, strong governance
- 10-12: CN Accountability 85-94, due diligence processes
- 7-9: CN Accountability 70-84, developing processes
- 4-6: Limited governance infrastructure
- 0-3: Weak accountability

**Deployment Capacity (0-10)** - Revenue (0-4) + Geographic (0-3) + Zakat (0-3)
- Revenue: $100M+=4, $25-100M=3, $5-25M=2, $1-5M=1, <$1M=0
- Geographic: National/international grantmaking=3, Regional=2, Local=1
- Zakat: Grantmaking for zakat causes can qualify (1-2 typical)

### Evidence of Impact (0-25) - 3 Sub-Components

**Output Tracking (0-8)** - Do they count what they do?
- 7-8: Grant tracking dashboards, portfolio metrics
- 5-6: Annual reports with grants, grantees
- 3-4: Basic grant counts
- 1-2: Anecdotal descriptions
- 0: No metrics

**Outcome Measurement (0-10)** - Grantee outcomes measured?
- 9-10: Portfolio-level impact, longitudinal grantee tracking
- 7-8: Grantee outcomes measured and reported
- 5-6: Some outcome indicators
- 3-4: Grant amounts only, no outcome measurement
- 0-2: No outcome tracking

**Third-Party Verification (0-7)** - Is there external validation?
- 6-7: Independent evaluations, grantee impact assessments
- 4-5: External reviews
- 2-3: Standard financial audit only
- 0-1: Limited external review

## CRITICAL CALIBRATION

**Ford Foundation sets the standard:**
- $750M+ in annual grantmaking
- Decades of field-building
- Global impact measurement
- This is what "world-class" philanthropy looks like

**Pillars Fund example:**
- Building Muslim philanthropic infrastructure = higher Ummah Gap (11-16)
- Smaller scale than major foundations = lower Systemic Leverage
- Appropriate range: 65-78 total score
