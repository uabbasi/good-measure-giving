Generate a baseline narrative for this charity with Wikipedia-style inline citations.

Write at an 8th grade reading level. Use short, clear sentences. Avoid jargon.
Do NOT use AI-sounding phrases like "It's important to note", "Furthermore",
"Moreover", "comprehensive approach", "robust framework", or "leveraging".
Write like a journalist, not a chatbot.

Use specific numbers, dates, and percentages from the data provided. Cite sources
with [N] markers that match the available sources listed below.

## CRITICAL: Do NOT reveal internal assessment scores

The pre-computed scores (AMAL score, impact score, alignment score, etc.) are
provided as CONTEXT for your analysis. They help you calibrate your tone. But do
NOT include these internal rating numbers in the narrative.

- BAD: "The charity earned an 81/100 AMAL score" or "Impact score of 37/50"
- BAD: "Alignment score of 44 out of 50" or "scored 81/100"
- GOOD: "This charity shows strong financial health but limited geographic reach"
- GOOD: "Donors can trust this group's spending, though it lacks outcome data"

Real financial data IS encouraged — use specific numbers from the data:
- GOOD: "$907 cost per beneficiary", "80% program expense ratio", "$147M revenue"
- GOOD: "100/100 Charity Navigator rating" (this is external, public data)

Explain the SUBSTANCE behind the assessments — what's strong, what's weak, and why.
